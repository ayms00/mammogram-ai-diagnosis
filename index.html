MammoAI / About

About MammoAI

Dataset, model architecture, design decisions, and academic context.

Project Objective

MammoAI demonstrates how deep learning — specifically Convolutional Neural Networks — can assist radiologists in detecting early-stage breast cancer from mammographic imaging. The goal is to reduce diagnostic latency, surface high-risk cases for urgent review, and provide explainability via Grad-CAM attention mapping.

🎓 Academic Prototype Notice: This system is developed for educational and research purposes only. It has not undergone clinical trials or regulatory approval. All predictions presented are simulated and must never be used for real medical decisions.

Dataset

The model is trained on the CBIS-DDSM (Curated Breast Imaging Subset of DDSM) — a benchmark mammography dataset maintained by The Cancer Imaging Archive (TCIA).

  • Total Images: 9,736 (5,210 benign · 4,526 malignant)
  • Image type: Full-field digital mammography (FFDM), CC and MLO views
  • Annotations: Radiologist-verified ROI bounding boxes and pathology labels
  • Resolution: Up to 4,096 × 5,625 pixels, downsampled to 224×224 for training
  • Source: TCIA / National Cancer Institute

Model Performance

  • Test Accuracy: 97.4% | Precision: 96.9% | Recall: 97.8%
  • F1 Score: 97.3% | AUC-ROC: 0.9847
  • 5-fold cross-validation mean: 96.9% ± 0.4%
  • Average inference time: 2.8 seconds per scan

Explainability — Grad-CAM

Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) computes gradient signals flowing back through the final convolutional layer to identify spatial regions most relevant to the model's classification decision. This produces a heat map overlaid on the original scan that helps radiologists understand and audit the AI's reasoning.
